PN Junction Experiment Details

Objective

To investigate the V-I characteristics of a PN junction diode under forward and reverse bias, and understand its behavior in electronic circuits.

Theory

A PN junction diode is formed when p-type and n-type semiconductors are joined. In forward bias, it allows current to flow easily, whereas in reverse bias, it blocks the current. This behavior is due to the movement of charge carriers and the formation of a depletion region at the junction. Understanding this concept is fundamental in designing circuits like rectifiers, voltage regulators, and switches.

  • Depletion Region: Region without free charge carriers that forms a barrier to current flow.
  • Forward Bias: P-side connected to positive terminal, reducing barrier and allowing current flow.
  • Reverse Bias: P-side connected to negative terminal, increasing barrier and restricting current flow.
  • Breakdown Voltage: Voltage at which the diode conducts heavily in reverse, possibly damaging it.
  • Applications: Used in rectifiers, voltage regulators, clippers, and signal demodulation circuits.

Apparatus Required

  • PN junction diode
  • DC power supply
  • Resistors (1kΩ, 100Ω)
  • Ammeter and Voltmeter (Digital/Analog)
  • Breadboard and connecting wires
  • Multimeter
